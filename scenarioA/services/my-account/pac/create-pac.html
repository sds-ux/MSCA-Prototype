The Social Insurance Number is a nine-digit number used in the administration of various Canadian government programs. Please enter all nine digits as they appear on your Social Insurance Number card, with no spaces.

Enter your first name (given name) as shown on your identification documents (i.e. SIN, passport, citizenship documentation). This information will be checked against the information in the Social Insurance Number record. Do not enter your initials, your nickname, or your family name in this field.

Please enter only your last name. This information will be checked against the information in the Social Insurance Number record.

The year, month and day of birth fields are mandatory. The month and day of birth must be selected from the drop-down lists provided, and the year of birth must contain four digits. For example, if you were born in 1947, please enter 1947 rather than 47.

Please enter only ONE of your parent’s last names as it was at the time of their birth.

DO NOT ENTER:

  • more than one of your parent’s last names; or
  • your parent’s married name, first name, middle name or date of birth.

Your parent may be a birth parent, adoptive parent, or legal guardian, as used in your Social Insurance Number application.

This information will be verified against the information in the Social Insurance Registry.

If you receive an error message, please try a different parent’s last name at the time of their birth.

By choosing the "Clear" button, all information that you have entered will be deleted.

By choosing the "Continue" button, you are submitting your personal information and consenting to its collection, use, and disclosure in accordance with the Personal Information Collection Statement.
