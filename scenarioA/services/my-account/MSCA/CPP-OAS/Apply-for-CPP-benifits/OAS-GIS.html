Step 1 of 3 - Apply for my Old Age Security (OAS) pension and the Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)

* When do you want your OAS pension to start?

Your Old Age Security (OAS) pension can start the month after your 65th birthday, or you can defer it. You can apply for it online up to 11 months before you want it to start.

To decide when you want your pension to start, think about your situation, your current and future sources of income, your work now and in the future and your plans for retirement. The Canadian Retirement Income Calculator can help you decide by giving you an estimate of how much you might receive.

For more information on OAS pension start date, see help or visit Canada.ca/OAS.

Applying for the Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)

If you filed your income tax for last year, Service Canada will get your income information from the Canada Revenue Agency to determine if you are eligible for the Guaranteed Income Supplement.

Every year that you file your income tax with the Canada Revenue Agency, we will review your income to see if you are eligible. If you are, we will add the Supplement to your monthly OAS pension amount.

* Legal status and residence history:

To be eligible for the OAS pension, Guaranteed Income Supplement, Allowance or Allowance for the Survivor, you must have legal status in Canada and you must meet the residence requirement. If living in Canada, you must be a Canadian citizen or legal resident when we approve your OAS application; if living outside Canada, you must have been a Canadian citizen or legal resident of Canada when you left.

* Do you want Service Canada to deduct federal tax from your monthly payment?

The OAS pension is taxable income. Income tax is only deducted from your monthly payment if you request it.

To request that income tax be deducted from your pension, or to change the amount being deducted online, through My Service Canada Account, select “Yes” below.
